ProgressiveBlurHeader gives you a native-quality sticky header with progressive blur in a single SwiftUI view. Content scrolls freely underneath the header, becoming increasingly blurred and tinted as it passes through — exactly like Apple Music, the App Store, and Photos. No UIKit wrappers, no clipping, no fuss.

## Why ProgressiveBlurHeader?

Building a progressive blur header from scratch in SwiftUI is surprisingly hard. There's no native API for variable-radius blur, and most attempts end up with clipped content, flat uniform blur, or brittle `UIViewRepresentable` wrappers that fight SwiftUI's layout system.

ProgressiveBlurHeader solves this with a three-layer `ZStack` approach:

- **Back layer** — a `ScrollView` that lets content scroll freely, never clipped
- **Middle layer** — a `VariableBlurView` with gradient tint that creates the progressive blur effect
- **Front layer** — your header, floating above the blur with no opaque background needed

The blur engine uses the same obfuscated private API that Apple uses in its own apps — the [VariableBlur](https://github.com/nikstar/VariableBlur) package by nikstar, which is App Store approved.

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add the package">
    In Xcode, go to **File → Add Package Dependencies** and paste `https://github.com/dominikmartn/ProgressiveBlurHeader`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import and use">
    Import `ProgressiveBlurHeader` and wrap your header and content in `StickyBlurHeader`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customize">
    Adjust `maxBlurRadius`, `fadeExtension`, and tint opacity to match your app's design language.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  ProgressiveBlurHeader requires iOS 16+ and Swift 5.9+.
</Note>
